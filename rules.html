Rules

Cards you are allowed to play

  1. Archetypal Cards: According to the given name of your PDS Slice, you are allowed to play any cards belonging to that slice. The general guideline is to search for the archetypes in the EDOPro search engine (unless stated otherwise), but the best way to see which card you are allowed to use is by using ⁠resource.
  2. Emperor Supports: After choosing a monster in your archetype as your Emperor, you will get to play
    • Generic Monster, Spell, or Trap that directly support the Attribute, Type, and/or Card Type of your emperor in its text (You can see the list of usable cards in ⁠resource).
    • Any Normal Monster that shares both the Attribute and Type of the Emperor.
    • The Kaiju Files: You can also choose to play up to a playset of the Kaiju that shared the same attribute as your Emperor (in the case of LIGHT, Jizukiru is the selected option).
    Emperor Supports CANNOT be chosen as an Emperor.
  3. Additional Inclusions: Additional Inclusions are cards that are specifically mentioned on the text of an archetypal card (including as its Summoning Condition) but does not belong to that archetype.
    • e.g. Torrential Tribute is mentioned in the text of Fury of Kairyu-Shin
    • e.g. Majestic Dragon is mentioned in the summoning condition of Majestic Red Dragon
    You are allowed to play Additional Inclusions in your Deck based on the allowed number on the PDS F&L List. Additional Inclusions CANNOT be chosen as an Emperor.
  4. Worthless Inclusions: Worthless Inclusions are unspecified cards that are required to fulfill the summoning condition or activation cost of an archetypal card.
    • e.g. a Machine "Cyber" with different attribute is required to activate the effect of Cybernetic Horizon in Cyberdark
    • e.g. a Deck with a Synchro Monster but no Tuner to use it
    You are allowed to play Worthless Inclusions in your Deck based on the allowed number on the PDS F&L List. Which card is considered to be the Worthless Inclusion will be decided by the people. Worthless Inclusions CANNOT be chosen as an Emperor.
    • e.g. Cyber Ogre (who is pretty useless) can be played in Cyberdark to activate Cybernetic Horizon.
    • e.g. a random Tuner(s) (preferably a vanilla with 0/0) with Level that is possible to become Synchro Materials for said Synchro Monster can be played. (If you play 3 of that Synchro Monster, you can play up to 3 worthless tuners, either 3 different ones or a playset)
  5. Miscellaneousaurus: When a card requires specific conditions that the archetype itself cannot fulfill, exceptions can be made on a case-by-case basis
    • e.g. You are allowed to have a full extra deck of random monsters to banish face-down in Nemleria
    • e.g. You are allowed to have cards in your Extra Deck (whose Level is higher than all the Nekroz monster) to activate Nekroz Kaleidoscope in Nekroz (up to the number of Nekroz Kaleidoscope you play)
    • e.g. You are allowed to have cards in Extra Deck to banish for the effect of Gizmek Orochi the Serpentron Sky Slasher in Gizmek (a playset for each copy of Orochi you play)
    • Decks with fusion monsters but no archetypal fusion spells are allowed to play 3 copies of Polymerization
    Make sure to always check ⁠resource to see which card belongs to which slice.

Cards you are NOT allowed to play

Unless the card is strictly banned on the PDS Forbidden List (you can see this in ⁠resource), you can play 03 copies of it. However, with good sportsmanship in mind, refrain from playing floodgates or cards that would generally make the game unenjoyable.

Deck Size Rules

Minimum deck size is 20, Maximum deck size is 40 with at least 15 cards belonging to the slice. The maximum count for Emperor support is 9 between your Main, Extra, and Side Deck. Both Worthless and Additional Inclusions are considered as Archetypal cards.

The Master Peace Treaty - the PDS F&L List

Cards that are among these categories are banned in PDS:

  • Cards that are (or would enable other cards to be) permanently unaffected by other cards' effects;
  • Cards with strong interruption/OTK capacity that are also (or would also enable other cards to be) temporarily unaffected by other cards' effects
  • Cards that have a mix of strong protection and interruptions/OTK capacity;
  • Cards with oppressive lingering floodgate effects (Restriction to Summons, Effects and/or Activations);
  • Cards that allow FTKs or obnoxious burn effects;
  • Generic cards that are generic endboard pieces (or enable generic endboard pieces) that can be made in most decks.

Cards that are among these categories are limited in PDS:

  • Cards that would cause unhealthy game state at multiple (handloop/deck-out, strong burn effect, cards that are (or would enable other cards to be) immune to responses, etc.);
  • Cards that have strong interruption/protection effect that can be made at multiple across a duel
  • Generic cards that are banned/limited in the TCG;
  • Generic endboard pieces with strong interruption or advantage-gaining effects
